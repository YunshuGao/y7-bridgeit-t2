Week 3 · Lesson 05

Meet the task + form pairs + write the design brief

Week 3 · 75 minutes · Year 7 Technology (Mandatory) · Bridge It

TE4-1DPTE4-2DP

🎯 Learning intentions

  • I can read and understand every section of the Bridge It Assessment Task.
  • I can choose a build partner and agree working arrangements.
  • I can write a design brief that answers WHAT, WHO, WHY, and CONSTRAINTS.

✅ Success criteria

  • I have signed the Assessment Task Acknowledgement.
  • My pair has a signed pair agreement in our folio.
  • Our shared design brief is pasted into the folio (min 3 sentences).

Do Now · 5 min

Silent 3-minute read — open the Assessment Task on the hub. Then write ONE question you have about the task in your folio. Ms Gao will answer all questions together.

I Do · ~10 min Teacher demonstration

Task walk-through. Ms Gao projects each section and reads aloud:

  1. Task title, task number, weighting
  2. Three parts + due dates
  3. Each part's requirements
  4. Bridge specifications (span, max size, allowed materials)
  5. Rubric — we read the A and C band for every criterion
  6. AI policy — grammar check only, no AI-generated sketches or evaluations
  7. Illness / misadventure procedure
This is real. Part A is due at the end of Lesson 14 (Week 7). Part B is the bridge at Lesson 20. Part C is the evaluation at Lesson 22. Put those dates in your diary right now.

We Do · ~15 min Guided practice

Design brief template — we write one together on the board as a class example:

DESIGN BRIEF
We are designing and building a ___ truss bridge made from popsicle sticks and PVA glue.
It will span a gap of 350 mm between two tables.
It must safely carry a hanging load of at least ___ grams.
The project must be complete by Lesson 20, using only the materials supplied.
Our design priorities are: strength, load-to-mass ratio, and neat construction.

You Do · ~35 min Independent pair work

Pair up + write YOUR brief (30 min).

  1. Pair formation — Ms Gao facilitates. You may request a partner; teacher has final say on pairings.
  2. Sign the pair agreement — together read the 4 clauses, both sign.
  3. Choose your truss type — from L04 (Pratt / Howe / Warren / K).
  4. Write your design brief — at least 3 sentences covering WHAT, WHO FOR, WHY, CONSTRAINTS.
  5. Paste the brief into your shared Google Doc folio (named Y7TECH-Bridge-[Your surnames].doc).

Differentiation

🪜Support

Sentence-starter template provided. Pair paired with Core student by teacher. Read-aloud of task sections.

🎯Core

Use template; fill in specifics for your pair's chosen truss type.

🚀Extension

Add a user persona — "Who is the end-user of a bridge like this?" Add 2 constraints beyond the minimum.

Exit Ticket · 5 min

Ticket out the door (Google Form):

  1. My partner is ___.
  2. Our truss type is ___.
  3. I understand that Part A is due in Lesson ___ (number).

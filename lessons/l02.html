Bridge Vocabulary + 4 Types Chart (PBS Building Big research)

Week 1 · 75 minutes · Year 7 Technology (Mandatory) · Bridge It

TE4-PDP-01TE4-DES-01TE4-DIG-01

🎯 Learning intentions

  • I can define 23 bridge vocabulary terms using the PBS Building Big glossary.
  • I can complete the 4-bridge-types research chart (beam, truss, arch, suspension).
  • I can build a simple index-card bridge that spans 20 cm and holds a pencil case.

✅ Success criteria

  • My Bridge Workbook Research Q1 (glossary) has all 23 definitions filled in.
  • My Workbook Research Q2 (chart) has illustrations + descriptions for all 4 bridge types.
  • I have built and tested an index-card bridge with my partner.

I Do · ~10 min Teacher demonstration

Today's research uses PBS Building Big — the authoritative online source for bridges. Ms Gao shows you how to navigate it on the projector.

🔗 PBS Building Big links you'll use:
1. Glossary: pbs.org/wgbh/buildingbig/glossary_head — for Workbook Q1
2. Bridge basics: pbs.org/wgbh/buildingbig/bridge/basics — for Workbook Q2

Teacher walks through the 4 bridge types with the PBS diagrams projected:

  1. Beam bridge — horizontal beam across supports. Simplest. Short spans only (<80 m). Example: concrete highway overpass.
  2. Truss bridge — triangular framework distributes load through all members. The type WE will build. Example: old steel rail bridges.
  3. Arch bridge — curved arch pushes load outward into abutments. Can hold huge loads. Example: Sydney Harbour Bridge (also has truss!).
  4. Suspension bridge — deck hangs from cables draped over tall towers. Huge spans (>1 km). Example: Golden Gate.
Key rule: longer span = more complex bridge type needed. Beam ≤ 80 m · Truss ≤ 150 m · Cable-stayed ≤ 1 km · Suspension ≤ 2 km.

We Do · ~15 min Guided practice

Together, we do Workbook Q1 — first 5 glossary terms (I demonstrate the technique, you follow):

  • Abutment — the support at each end of a bridge where it meets the ground.
  • Aqueduct — a bridge that carries water (often an old Roman one).
  • Arch Bridge — a bridge with a curved arch that transfers load to abutments.
  • Beam Bridge — simplest bridge; horizontal beam on two supports.
  • Brace — a structural member that strengthens or stiffens a frame.

Now Ms Gao shows you how to look up the remaining 18 terms yourself.

You Do · ~35 min Independent pair work

Part 1 — Workbook Q1 (15 min): complete the remaining 18 glossary terms yourself (Brittle · Buckle · Buttress · Cable-Stayed Bridge · Cantilever · Cement · Compression · Concrete · Deform · Masonry · Movable Bridge · Pier · Reinforced Concrete · Shear · Suspension Bridge · Tension · Torsion · Truss).

Part 2 — Workbook Q2 (10 min): complete the 4-bridge-types chart with an illustration + 1-sentence description for each type (Beam / Truss / Arch / Suspension). Use the PBS basics page.

Part 3 — Mini-build #1 — Index Card Bridge (15 min): with your partner, using 3 index cards + 30 cm masking tape, build a bridge that spans a 20 cm gap and holds a pencil case for 10 seconds.

  1. Plan — 3 min. Sketch in your workbook before building.
  2. Build — 8 min.
  3. Test — hang your pencil case on the deck. Did it hold?
  4. Reflect — what bridge TYPE did you just build? 2 sentences in workbook.
WHS: scissors out only for card-cutting. Pass handle-first. Cut away from body.

Differentiation

🪜Support

Provided pre-cut card strips and a photo of a beam-and-brace example. Pair with a Core student.

🎯Core

Build the index card bridge with no template — use your own design.

🚀Extension

After success, try to span 30 cm with the same 3 cards + tape. What had to change?

Exit Ticket · 5 min

Write in your folio — 3 sentences:

  1. Name the 5 bridge types.
  2. Which type would YOU build for a 500 m gap? Why?
  3. What bridge type did your index-card bridge act like today?

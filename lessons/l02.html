Welcome + Workbook Folio + Famous Bridges Tour + Glossary

Week 1 · 75 minutes · Year 7 Technology (Mandatory) · Bridge It

TE4-PDP-01TE4-DES-01TE4-SDP-01TE4-DIG-01

🎯 Learning intentions

  • I know how my tier (Support / Core / Extension) was set from the L01 diagnostic.
  • I can open my Bridge Workbook Folio and fill in the cover page.
  • I can identify 4 famous bridges and name 5 parts of a bridge (deck · pier · abutment · span · truss).
  • I can define 23 bridge vocabulary terms using the PBS Building Big glossary — Workbook Q1.

✅ Success criteria

  • My Bridge Workbook Folio has my name, class and date on the cover page.
  • I can label deck · pier · abutment · span · truss on a bridge diagram.
  • My Workbook Research Q1 has most of the 23 glossary terms defined.
  • I know which tier I'm placed in and what that means for how I'll be taught.

Do Now · 5 min

Tier reveal + materials check (3 min). Ms Gao hands back the L01 diagnostic with your tier sticker (Support 🪜 / Core 🎯 / Extension 🚀) on the front. No scores announced — tiers are PRIVATE between you and Ms Gao.

Remember: your tier is about HOW Ms Gao teaches you this term, not about who you are. Tiers can change during the term.

I Do · ~10 min Teacher demonstration

Part 1 — Welcome to the Bridge Workbook Folio (5 min). Meet the booklet you'll fill in across the whole term. Every research activity, sketch, PMI analysis, and evaluation lives here. You can print it OR use the interactive HTML version on your laptop — your choice.

What's inside your Workbook Folio (9 sections across 11 weeks):
  1. Design Brief (L05)
  2. Specification (L05)
  3. Research — glossary, 4 types, Tacoma, SHB (L02–L06)
  4. Generation of Ideas — 3 sketches (L07)
  5. PMI Analysis (L07)
  6. Manufacturing Process (L11)
  7. Destruction Video (L20)
  8. Strength-to-Weight Ratio (L20)
  9. Evaluation (L21–L22)
Sections unlock as lessons reach them. Today we only need the cover + Research §3 Q1.

Part 2 — Famous bridges tour (5 min). As Ms Gao projects each, note 1 fact in your workbook:

  • Sydney Harbour Bridge, Australia — 1932, steel arch + truss, 503 m span.
  • Golden Gate Bridge, USA — 1937, suspension, "International Orange".
  • Millau Viaduct, France — 2004, cable-stayed, taller than Eiffel Tower.
  • Akashi-Kaikyō Bridge, Japan — 1998, suspension, longest in the world.
5 parts of a bridge (label on the diagram in your workbook):
deck — road/walking surface · pier — vertical tower · abutment — where bridge meets the ground · span — the gap · truss — triangular structural frame.

We Do · ~15 min Guided practice

Workbook Q1 launch — first 5 glossary terms together (10 min). Ms Gao opens the PBS Building Big Glossary on the projector and demonstrates the technique:

  1. Click the term on the PBS page.
  2. Read the definition carefully.
  3. Write the definition IN YOUR OWN WORDS in your workbook (don't copy-paste — paraphrase).
  4. If the PBS definition uses a hard word, look that one up too.

Together we do these 5:

  • Abutment — the support at each end of a bridge where it meets the ground.
  • Aqueduct — a bridge that carries water (often a Roman one).
  • Arch Bridge — a bridge with a curved arch that transfers load to abutments.
  • Beam Bridge — simplest bridge; horizontal beam on two supports.
  • Brace — a structural member that strengthens or stiffens a frame.

You Do · ~35 min Independent pair work

Workbook Q1 — remaining 18 glossary terms (35 min).

Open the PBS Building Big Glossary on your laptop and define the remaining terms in your workbook. Work quietly. You may sit with your elbow-partner but each of you fills in YOUR OWN workbook.

Terms to define: Brittle · Buckle · Buttress · Cable-Stayed Bridge · Cantilever · Cement · Compression · Concrete · Deform · Masonry · Movable Bridge · Pier · Reinforced Concrete · Shear · Suspension Bridge · Tension · Torsion · Truss.

Workbook tip: if you're using the interactive version, your answers save automatically. If you're writing on paper, you'll type them up for homework this week.

Workbook Q2 (if you finish Q1 early) — 4 bridge types chart. Fill in illustrations + 1-sentence descriptions for Beam · Truss · Arch · Suspension. Use the PBS Bridge Basics page. (Most pairs will finish Q2 in L03, that's fine.)

Differentiation

🪜Support

Provided pre-cut card strips and a photo of a beam-and-brace example. Pair with a Core student.

🎯Core

Build the index card bridge with no template — use your own design.

🚀Extension

After success, try to span 30 cm with the same 3 cards + tape. What had to change?

Exit Ticket · 5 min

Write in your folio — 3 sentences:

  1. Name the 5 bridge types.
  2. Which type would YOU build for a 500 m gap? Why?
  3. What bridge type did your index-card bridge act like today?

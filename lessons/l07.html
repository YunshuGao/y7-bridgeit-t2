Week 4 · Lesson 07

Idea generation — 3 thumbnail sketches

Week 4 · 75 minutes · Year 7 Technology (Mandatory) · Bridge It

TE4-1DP

🎯 Learning intentions

  • I can generate 3 different bridge design ideas using the SCAMPER technique.
  • I can write pros and cons for each idea.
  • I can give my partner constructive feedback using the Elements & Principles of Design (from Term 1).

✅ Success criteria

  • My folio has 3 labelled thumbnail sketches.
  • Each sketch has 2 pros and 2 cons.
  • I have given my partner written feedback on each of their sketches.

Do Now · 5 min

Look at your research from Lesson 6. In 2 minutes, write in your folio:

  1. One feature I like from a bridge in my research.
  2. One feature I would change.
  3. One colour/pattern idea for my bridge.

I Do · ~10 min Teacher demonstration

Teacher introduces SCAMPER — a technique to generate ideas by changing one thing at a time:

  • Substitute — replace popsicle sticks with bamboo skewers in your head. What changes?
  • Combine — combine a Warren and a Pratt. What would that look like?
  • Adapt — adapt an arch bridge idea for our popsicle stick constraints.
  • Modify — make the bridge taller / wider / thicker.
  • Put to another use — what if our bridge had to carry a train instead of a bucket?
  • Eliminate — can you remove the verticals? What happens?
  • Reverse — flip the truss pattern upside down.

Teacher sketches 3 different bridge ideas live on the board, each using a different SCAMPER operation, with pros/cons.

We Do · ~15 min Guided practice

Together we evaluate one of the teacher's sketches using the Elements & Principles of Design from Term 1:

  • Elements — does it use line, shape, form well?
  • Principles — is it balanced? Does it have rhythm (repeating triangles)?
  • Function — will it hold weight?
  • Aesthetics — does it look good?

You Do · ~35 min Independent pair work

3 sketches + pair peer review (30 min).

  1. You (individually in your pair, both partners sketching): 3 thumbnail sketches using 3 different SCAMPER operations. Each sketch about half a page, pencil, labelled.
  2. For each sketch, write 2 pros and 2 cons.
  3. Swap folios with ANOTHER pair. Give written feedback on each of their sketches:
    • One thing they did well (Element/Principle language)
    • One improvement suggestion
  4. With your partner, pick your favourite sketch — this is the one you'll progress to scaled drawing next lesson.

Differentiation

🪜Support

Pre-drawn truss outline — you modify rather than draw from scratch. Pros/cons sentence starters provided.

🎯Core

3 original sketches with written pros/cons.

🚀Extension

Add a small cost-benefit table for each sketch (sticks used, expected strength, difficulty).

Exit Ticket · 5 min

  1. Which SCAMPER operation helped you most today?
  2. Which of your 3 sketches did you choose for the final, and why?

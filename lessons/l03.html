Week 2 · Lesson 03

Forces up close — where compression and tension live

Week 2 · 75 minutes · Year 7 Technology (Mandatory) · Bridge It

TE4-8ENTE4-9MA

🎯 Learning intentions

  • I can identify compression and tension in a real bridge.
  • I can predict where a paper beam will bend before I test it.
  • I can use the words compression, tension, bending, and deflection correctly.

✅ Success criteria

  • My folio has a labelled force diagram of a beam bridge under load.
  • I have completed the paper beam deflection experiment with my partner.
  • I can explain why the TOP of a loaded beam compresses and the BOTTOM stretches.

Do Now · 5 min

From Term 1 L05 — quick recall:

📝 Folio zone

  1. Compression is a ___ force (one word).
  2. Tension is a ___ force (one word).
  3. Name one place in your body right now where compression acts.
  4. Name one place where tension acts.

I Do · ~10 min Teacher demonstration

Teacher demo — the paper beam under load.

  1. Place a single A4 paper flat across two chairs (the "beam"). Add a book. Watch it collapse.
  2. Fold the same paper lengthwise (concertina shape). Place across two chairs. Add the book. Watch it hold.

Why the concertina works: folds give the paper DEPTH. The top of the beam compresses slightly; the bottom stretches slightly. The more depth, the less bending. This is why real beams are I-shaped — depth without weight.

New words today:
bending = when a beam curves under load
deflection = how much a beam sags in the middle
shear = when two parts of a structure try to slide past each other

We Do · ~15 min Guided practice

Together, we draw a beam bridge with a truck on top. Mark:

  1. Where is compression? (Hint: the TOP half of the beam.)
  2. Where is tension? (The BOTTOM half of the beam.)
  3. Where is the biggest bending force? (Middle of the span.)

Now a truss bridge. The truss turns the bending of a beam into straight compression and tension in each member — that's why trusses carry more load for the same weight.

You Do · ~35 min Independent pair work

Mini-build #2 — Paper Beam Deflection Experiment (25 min)

In pairs. You have 5 sheets of A4 paper, 30 cm masking tape, a ruler, and a 200 g weight (or a book).

  1. Test 1 — single flat sheet across 20 cm gap. Add weight to centre. Measure how far it sags (deflection in mm). Record.
  2. Test 2 — fold one sheet concertina-style. Repeat. Record deflection.
  3. Test 3 — roll one sheet into a tube. Repeat. Record deflection.
  4. Test 4 — stack 3 flat sheets. Repeat. Record.

In your folio table: record deflection in mm for each test. Then in 2 sentences — which shape had the LEAST deflection? Why?

Differentiation

🪜Support

Pre-printed results table. Pair with Core student. Focus on Tests 1 & 2 only.

🎯Core

All 4 tests with table and 2-sentence reflection.

🚀Extension

Add Test 5 — fold paper into a tube AND add a diagonal brace. Predict AND test whether deflection decreases further.

Exit Ticket · 5 min

On a sticky note:

  1. Draw a beam with a heavy weight in the middle. Label one spot where compression acts and one spot where tension acts.
  2. Finish this sentence: "The deeper a beam, the ___ it bends."

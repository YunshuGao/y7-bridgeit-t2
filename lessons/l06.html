Week 3 · Lesson 06

Research — truss types, famous bridges, and civil engineers

Week 3 · 75 minutes · Year 7 Technology (Mandatory) · Bridge It

TE4-1DPTE4-10TS

🎯 Learning intentions

  • I can find reliable sources online and recognise unreliable ones.
  • I can annotate a source with a 1-sentence note saying WHY it's useful.
  • I can explain what a civil engineer does and give one real example.

✅ Success criteria

  • My folio has 6 annotated sources (2+ bridge types, 2+ truss types, 1 famous bridge, 1 engineer career).
  • Each annotation is 1 sentence explaining why I chose the source.
  • I know at least one real civil engineer's name and one bridge they worked on.

Do Now · 5 min

Quick reliability check. Which of these sources is MOST reliable for bridge research? Rank 1 (most) to 4 (least):

  1. A random Instagram post about bridges
  2. Engineers Australia website
  3. A YouTube video with 50 million views from an unnamed channel
  4. Britannica encyclopedia online

I Do · ~10 min Teacher demonstration

Teacher models annotating a source. Project a page from Engineers Australia on a bridge. Show:

  • Copy the URL into your folio
  • Read 2 paragraphs
  • Write 1 sentence: "I'm using this because it explains ___ which I need for ___."
3 reliability signs: author named · organisation recognisable · up-to-date · no spelling errors · has references itself.

Career spotlight — civil engineer. Civil engineers design the structures that keep society moving: roads, bridges, dams, tunnels, water supply. In Australia they study a 4-year B.Eng at university. Local example: Dr JJC Bradfield designed the Sydney Harbour Bridge.

We Do · ~15 min Guided practice

Together we fill in the first 2 rows of the annotation table:

You Do · ~35 min Independent pair work

Research session — 30 minutes of focused reading.

Find and annotate 6 sources. Distribution:

  • 2 sources on bridge TYPES (beam / arch / truss / suspension / cable-stayed)
  • 2 sources on TRUSS TYPES (Pratt / Howe / Warren / K)
  • 1 source on a famous bridge (choose your own)
  • 1 source on a civil engineer's career or profile
Not allowed: AI-generated text as a source. Tiktok. Instagram. Anonymous blogs.

Paste URLs + 1-sentence annotations into your folio annotation table.

Differentiation

🪜Support

3 pre-vetted source URLs provided. Sentence-starter: "I'm using this source because it explains ___."

🎯Core

Find all 6 sources with teacher check-in after 15 min.

🚀Extension

Find a source in a language other than English (use translation tool). Compare construction methods between countries.

Exit Ticket · 5 min

  1. Name one civil engineer you researched today.
  2. Name the bridge they are most known for.
  3. Which of your 6 sources do you think is the MOST useful and why?

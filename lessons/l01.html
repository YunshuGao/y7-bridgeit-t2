Welcome to Bridge It + the Bridge Workbook Folio + Diagnostic

Week 1 · 75 minutes · Year 7 Technology (Mandatory) · Bridge It

TE4-PDP-01TE4-DES-01TE4-SDP-01

🎯 Learning intentions

  • I can describe what the Bridge It unit will ask me to do across 11 weeks.
  • I can open my Bridge Workbook Folio and fill in my name + class on the cover.
  • I can identify 4 famous bridges (Sydney Harbour, Golden Gate, Millau, Akashi-Kaikyō).
  • I can name 5 parts of a bridge using the correct vocabulary (deck, pier, abutment, span, truss).

✅ Success criteria

  • My Bridge Workbook Folio has my name, class and date on the cover page.
  • I can label deck · pier · abutment · span · truss on a bridge diagram.
  • I know my Design Folio (Part A) is due end of Lesson 14 / Week 7.
  • I have taken (or received my mark for) the 40-mark Bridge It Diagnostic.

I Do · ~10 min Teacher demonstration

Meet your Bridge Workbook Folio — the booklet you will fill in across the whole term. Every research activity, design idea, sketch, PMI analysis, and evaluation lives here. You print it OR use the interactive HTML version on your laptop — your choice.

What's inside your Workbook Folio (9 sections across 11 weeks):
  1. Design Brief (L05)
  2. Specification (L05)
  3. Research — bridge glossary, 4 bridge types, PBS challenge, Tacoma Narrows, Sydney Harbour Bridge (L02–L06)
  4. Generation of Ideas — 3 sketches (L07)
  5. PMI Analysis — Plus / Minus / Interesting (L07)
  6. Manufacturing Process — procedural text (L11)
  7. Destruction Video — your load test (L20)
  8. Strength-to-Weight Ratio (L20)
  9. Evaluation (L21–L22)

Teacher-led tour of four world-famous bridges. As Ms Gao projects each one, fill in the Bridges Introduction section of your workbook:

  • Sydney Harbour Bridge, Australia — 1932, steel arch + truss hybrid, 503 m span, built from both sides to meet in the middle.
  • Golden Gate Bridge, USA — 1937, suspension, 1,280 m main span, "International Orange" colour.
  • Millau Viaduct, France — 2004, cable-stayed, 2,460 m long, taller than the Eiffel Tower at its highest pier.
  • Akashi-Kaikyō Bridge, Japan — 1998, suspension, 1,991 m main span. Survived a 7.2 earthquake during construction.
5 parts of a bridge (label these on the diagram in your workbook):
deck — the road/walking surface.   pier — the vertical support tower standing in the water or gorge.   abutment — where the bridge MEETS the ground (at the ends).   span — the gap between two supports.   truss — the triangular structural frame.

We Do · ~15 min Guided practice

On your laptop, open Google Maps. Together we'll fly over:

  1. Parramatta — find a local bridge near our school. Zoom in. What type does it look like?
  2. Sydney — Sydney Harbour Bridge. Zoom in on the truss pattern. Count the triangles you can see.
  3. One you choose — a bridge anywhere in the world that looks interesting to you.

Class discussion: what is the ONE thing all bridges must do? (Answer: carry a load across a gap without falling down. Everything else is design choice.)

You Do · ~35 min Independent pair work

Part 1 — Bridge It Diagnostic Test (40 marks, 50 minutes)

Ms Gao hands out the paper test. Use pencil + ruler. Don't stress — your mark tells me WHAT TO TEACH YOU, not what you should already know. Your tier (Support / Core / Extension) will be posted in Google Classroom by Friday.

Part 2 — If you finish early: open your Bridge Workbook Folio and start filling in page 1 (cover) and page 2 (favourite bridge from today's tour — 5 bullet facts).

Differentiation

🪜Support

Paired reading of the diagnostic instructions. Vocabulary cards with pictures for deck/pier/abutment/span/truss. Pre-typed workbook cover page to fill in.

🎯Core

Complete diagnostic independently. Fill in cover + bridges intro in workbook.

🚀Extension

After diagnostic + cover, research ONE bridge not covered in the tour — fill in the 'Extra Bridge' page with 5 facts + 1 engineering question you'd ask its designer.

Exit Ticket · 5 min

On a sticky note OR in your workbook exit ticket box:

  2. What date is my Design Folio (Part A) due?
  3. Which of the 9 Workbook Folio sections am I most nervous about?

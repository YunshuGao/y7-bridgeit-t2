I Do · ~10 min Teacher demonstration

Welcome to Term 2 Technology (5 min).

Very quick overview — just so you know what's coming:

  • Term 2 unit = Bridge It. 11 weeks. You'll build a real popsicle-stick bridge.
  • You'll work in pairs for most of the term.
  • Final bridge will be load-tested until it breaks (it's supposed to — that's the fun part 🌉💥).
  • One big assessment task — released in Lesson 5.
Why we're testing TODAY before teaching ANYTHING: if I teach you about bridges first, then test you, I'm really testing whether you were paying attention — not what you already know. So we test FIRST. The score tells me where YOU are so I know what I need to teach YOU.

What's on the test:

  • Part A — 10 marks: recognise things in bridge photos (you'll be guessing on some — that's OK)
  • Part B — 10 marks: short written answers about forces and design
  • Part C — 9 marks: reading a scaled drawing (you've done scale in Term 1)
  • Part D — 11 marks: freehand drawing — cube + truss sketch

Total 40 marks. 50 minutes. No phones, no laptops, no talking. Skip anything you're unsure of.

We Do · ~15 min Guided practice

Quick test-taking reminders (5 min). Ms Gao reads these aloud:

  1. Write your name on the front page.
  2. Use pencil so you can rub out and redraw.
  3. Read each question once before writing. Don't panic.
  4. Skip questions you don't know and come back at the end. Don't sit on one question for more than 2 minutes.
  5. For Part D (drawing), use your ruler. Don't worry about making it perfect.
  6. When you finish, silently raise your hand and I'll collect. You may read quietly until everyone is done.

Any questions about the test instructions? (Not the content — I can't help with answers.)

Differentiation

🪜Support

Ms Gao reads Part B + Part C prompts aloud (instructions only, not the answers). Larger-print test version available on request. No time pressure beyond the 50 min — you stop when the bell goes.

🎯Core

Standard test conditions. Independent work, silent room.

🚀Extension

If you finish early (rare), no extra tasks. Read quietly until the bell. Use the extra time to double-check Part D drawings — is your cube closed? Are diagonals even?

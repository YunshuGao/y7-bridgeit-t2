Welcome to Bridge It — bridges everywhere

Week 1 · 75 minutes · Year 7 Technology (Mandatory) · Bridge It

TE4-1DPTE4-10TS

🎯 Learning intentions

  • I can describe what the Bridge It unit will ask me to do across 11 weeks.
  • I can identify 4 famous bridges and explain where they are in the world.
  • I can name 5 parts of a bridge using the correct vocabulary.

✅ Success criteria

  • My folio has a dated Week 1 Lesson 1 entry.
  • I can label deck, pier, abutment, span and truss on a bridge diagram.
  • I know when Part A of my assessment is due (end of Lesson 14 / Week 7).

Do Now · 5 min

Bridges you already know — brainstorm with your table group for 3 minutes:

  1. Name every bridge you have been on in your life. Go.
  2. Pick your favourite and write ONE sentence saying why it stuck in your memory.

📝 Folio zone

Bridges I have crossed: ________________________

Favourite bridge and why: ________________________

I Do · ~10 min Teacher demonstration

Teacher-led tour of four world-famous bridges. As Ms Gao projects each one, note in your folio: where is it, what type, what's special.

  • Sydney Harbour Bridge, Australia — 1932, steel arch + truss hybrid, 503 m span, built from both sides to meet in the middle (remember Term 1 L07? That's why measurement matters!)
  • Golden Gate Bridge, USA — 1937, suspension, 1,280 m main span, the orange-red colour is "International Orange".
  • Millau Viaduct, France — 2004, cable-stayed, 2,460 m long, taller than the Eiffel Tower at its highest pier.
  • Akashi-Kaikyō Bridge, Japan — 1998, suspension, 1,991 m main span — longest in the world. Survived a 7.2 earthquake during construction.
Key vocabulary (label these in your folio): deck (the road surface) · pier (vertical support tower) · abutment (where the bridge meets the ground) · span (the gap between supports) · truss (the triangular structural frame).

We Do · ~15 min Guided practice

On your laptop, open Google Maps. Together we'll fly over:

  1. Parramatta — find a local bridge near our school. Zoom in. What type does it look like?
  2. Sydney — Sydney Harbour Bridge. Zoom in on the truss pattern. Count the triangles you can see.
  3. One you choose — a bridge anywhere in the world that looks interesting to you.

Class discussion: what is the ONE thing all bridges must do? (Answer: carry a load across a gap without falling down. Everything else is design choice.)

You Do · ~35 min Independent pair work

Your big 11-week project. Open the Bridge It task on the term2 page. Read it once on your own. Then with a partner, write answers in your folio:

  1. How many weeks is this unit?
  2. How many parts does the assessment have, and what percent is each?
  3. What material will we build with?
  4. When is Part A due?
  5. What are you most excited about in this unit?
  6. What are you most worried about? (Honest answers help Ms Gao help you.)

Then: open the famous bridges gallery and choose ONE bridge (not one from the I Do tour) to research for 15 minutes. Write 5 bullet facts in your folio.

Differentiation

🪜Support

Partner-up for reading the task — buddy system. Vocabulary cards provided with pictures for deck/pier/abutment/span/truss. Pre-typed folio template.

🎯Core

Complete all 6 questions + 5 bullet facts on chosen bridge.

🚀Extension

Research TWO bridges (one ancient, one modern). Compare their construction methods in a short paragraph.

Exit Ticket · 5 min

One sentence each on a sticky note or in your folio:

  1. What is the longest bridge in the world? (Answer from today's lesson.)
  2. What date is my Design Folio (Part A) due?
  3. What is one thing I want to learn in Bridge It?

Week 2 · Lesson 04

Why triangles win — truss geometry

Week 2 · 75 minutes · Year 7 Technology (Mandatory) · Bridge It

TE4-8EN

🎯 Learning intentions

  • I can name four common truss types — Pratt, Howe, Warren, K-truss.
  • I can identify a truss type from a photograph.
  • I can explain why a triangle cannot be deformed without breaking a side.

✅ Success criteria

  • My folio has labelled sketches of the 4 truss types.
  • I scored at least 6/10 on the diagnostic truss quiz.
  • I can colour-code compression (red) and tension (blue) members in a Pratt truss.

Do Now · 5 min

The classic rigidity test. Teacher hands each pair 4 popsicle sticks and 4 push-pins (or tape).

  1. Make a square. Push on one corner. What happens?
  2. Add a 5th stick across the diagonal. Push again. What happens now?

📝 Folio zone

A square alone is ___. A square with a diagonal becomes ___ triangles, and is now ___.

I Do · ~10 min Teacher demonstration

Teacher draws each truss type on the board. You copy into your folio.

  • Pratt truss — diagonal members slope DOWN toward the centre. Verticals are in compression, diagonals in tension. Cheap, common, easy.
  • Howe truss — diagonal members slope UP toward the centre. Verticals in tension, diagonals in compression. Opposite of Pratt.
  • Warren truss — alternating diagonals form equilateral triangles (no verticals). Elegant and light.
  • K-truss — diagonals meet at the midpoint of verticals, forming K shapes. Used for very tall or wide trusses where normal diagonals would be too long.

Real-world examples: Sydney Harbour Bridge is a Pratt-style truss inside its arch. The Eiffel Tower uses Warren and K-trusses together.

We Do · ~15 min Guided practice

Photo identification round. Ms Gao projects 5 bridge photos. Call out as a class which truss each one is. Then colour-code force direction on a Pratt truss together:

  • Top chord (horizontal, top) — RED = compression
  • Bottom chord (horizontal, bottom) — BLUE = tension
  • Verticals — depends on the type (Pratt: compression; Howe: tension)
  • Diagonals — the opposite of verticals

You Do · ~35 min Independent pair work

Diagnostic quiz + truss sketching (30 min). This quiz does NOT count toward your mark — it helps Ms Gao know what to re-teach.

  1. Complete the 10-question quiz on your laptop (link below).
  2. Sketch each of the 4 truss types to scale in your folio, using technical lettering for labels (remember Term 1 L10!).
  3. Colour-code the Pratt truss — red for compression, blue for tension, black for the load.
  4. With your partner: agree which truss type you think you might use for YOUR bridge. Write 2 sentences giving reasons.

Differentiation

🪜Support

Partial truss diagrams provided to colour in. Focus on Pratt and Warren only. Quiz read aloud by teacher.

🎯Core

Sketch all 4 truss types freehand with labels. Take the quiz independently.

🚀Extension

Research one advanced truss type (lenticular, K-truss variant, arch-truss hybrid). Sketch and annotate it.

Exit Ticket · 5 min

On a sticky note:

  1. A triangle cannot change shape unless ___ breaks.
  2. Which truss type did YOU pick for your bridge, and why?
  3. One thing from today's quiz you got wrong and now understand.
